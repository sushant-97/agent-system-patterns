AI, in motion.

Agent System Patterns · Showcase

SP
Sushant Pargaonkar June 2026 · 3 min read

A short detour from the usual notes. No framework, no verdict — just a few ideas from AI, drawn in motion. Every diagram on this page is plain SVG and CSS: no libraries, no canvas, no video. View source and take whatever's useful.

Words become vectors

Before a model can reason about language, each token is mapped to a point in a high-dimensional space. Here the tokens light up in turn and their meaning streams down into the cloud as glowing trails.

Attention: who looks at whom

A transformer lets every token decide how much to weigh every other. The thicker, brighter the arc, the more one word is paying attention to another. Watch the strongest link carry a pulse.

Learning is rolling downhill

Training is mostly one move repeated: nudge the weights in the direction that lowers the error, and roll a little further into the valley. The ghosts are where the ball just was — the path it carved getting here.

Watching a model train

Plot that error after every step and you get the shape every practitioner knows: jagged, impatient, but trending down. Here the curve draws itself, over and over.

The point

None of this is a library. Each diagram is a few SVG shapes plus a handful of CSS keyframes — flowing dashes for trails, motion paths for the moving dots, a dash offset to draw a line on its own. Cheap, sharp at any size, and kind to a phone battery. If a future note needs a diagram that moves, this is the toolkit.
